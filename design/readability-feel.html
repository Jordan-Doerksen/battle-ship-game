The Sound Board

Every cue below is pure synthesis — oscillators, filtered noise, gain envelopes. Zero samples. Each recipe is one small parameterized function (params documented in the source above it); approve here and the same numbers bake to WAV offline for Godot. Wired to the sim's existing effect events — including the two the renderer currently drops on the floor: klaxon and waveclear.

ALL QUIET — sound is garnish, never the message. Every cue duplicates something already visible (the klaxon has the wake dash, the ready-blink has the arc, the sting has the banner). Muted and reduced-motion players lose nothing; sound never carries information alone.

The Readability Board

Six small loops. Judge each on one question: can a tired operator read it at a glance, at zoom 0.5, mid-straddle?
1 · Torpedo on the scope
OLD: the torpedo was the same 1.6 px mote as every shell — illegible. NEW: a foam dash laid along its run + trailing wake sparks (the C5 spec's promised tell, finally paid). Same five shell motes on both discs.
2 · DC arm ring + rack
Dashed foam ring = depth-charge arm radius (220 u). Solid dim ring = sonar (350 u) — never confusable. The rack arc refills over 4 s by the blip, then blinks ready. Nobody counts in their head anymore.
3 · Wounded tells
Below half hp: a 7° list, a darkened hull, a drifting smoke wisp. At the last pip: a flicker of flame. Read the boat, not a health bar.
4 · Pause
Pause holds the war, not the water — the sea drifts on beneath the plate, by design. The sim clock stops; the cosmetic clock does not.
5 · Lost-card guard
For 1.5 s the lost card ignores every click — no accidental restart mid-flinch. Then the keys appear. The mouse stays ignored forever; R / T only.
6 · Hint plates — contextual drip
